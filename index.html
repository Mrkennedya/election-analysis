Breaking Down Alachua County Election Results by Demographics

By Angel Kennedy // University of Florida

Updated Dec. 10, 2018 (v1.0)

In 2018, turnout meant everything.

(Click anywhere to continue.)

The 2018 midterm elections produced some of the highest rates of voter turnout in the country.

In Florida, the trend was similar: More than eight million voters turned out to the polls to vote for a new governor and senator, shaking up the political landscape with several toss-up races and flipped political seats.

Even in Alachua County – one of the state's few Democratic hotbeds – voters of various ages, ethnicities and political parties accounted for the county's highest turnout rate in over 20 years.

Election data from the Alachua County Supervisor of Elections was used to break down the county's election results into three demographics – age, ethnicity and political party.
